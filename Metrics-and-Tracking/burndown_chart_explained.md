# Burndown Chart – Meaning & Usage

## What a Burndown Chart Shows
- Remaining work over time
- Progress toward Sprint Goal
- Early signals of risk

## How to Read It
- Ideal line: Expected progress
- Actual line: Real progress

## Healthy Patterns
- Gradual downward trend
- Small variances corrected early

## Warning Signs
- Flat line for many days (blocked work)
- Sharp drops near sprint end (late testing)
- Frequent scope additions mid-sprint

## Scrum Master Role
- Use burndown to **ask questions**
- Never use it to blame or pressure

## Scrum Master Insight
Metrics are for learning and transparency,
not for control or comparison.
