# Sprint Planning – Practical Template

## Purpose of Sprint Planning
Sprint Planning ensures the team commits to
**a realistic, valuable, and achievable goal** for the sprint.

## Step 1: Define Sprint Goal
Sprint Goal should answer:
“What business or user value will this sprint deliver?”

Example:
Improve system stability and diagnostics for operators.

## Step 2: Review Priorities
- Review top backlog items
- Clarify acceptance criteria
- Discuss dependencies and risks

## Step 3: Capacity Planning
- Account for holidays, on-call duties, support work
- Avoid over-commitment
- Quality over quantity

## Step 4: Task Breakdown
- Break stories into tasks
- Identify unclear or risky areas early
- Encourage team ownership

## Step 5: Commitment
Team commits collectively to the Sprint Goal,
not to individual tasks.

## Scrum Master Insight
Sprint planning is successful when the team
feels confident — not pressured — about delivery.
